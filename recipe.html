My Mom's Lumpia Recipe


Lumpia are a type of spring roll from Filipino cuisine, consisting of a thin wrapper filled with savory or sweet ingredients like ground pork and vegetables. They can be served fresh or deep-fried until crispy and are often enjoyed as an appetizer or snack at gatherings. Lumpia has its origins in ancient China, introduced to the Philippines by Chinese immigrants centuries ago. The name itself comes from the Hokkien words "lun" (moist) and "pia" (pastry). Over time, it was adapted to local tastes and ingredients, becoming a staple in Filipino cuisine.


Instructions

  1. First is to combine all the filling ingredients in a bowl, and then mix them altogether until well blended.
  2. The next step is to wrap the lumpia mixture in egg roll wrapper. You can also use the traditional lumpia wrapper for a more authentic result.
  3. The final step is to fry it until brown and crispy (deep fry)

Ingredients

  • 50 pieces lumpia wrapper
  • 3 cups cooking oil

Filling Ingredients

  • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork
  • 2 pieces onion minced
  • 2 pieces carrots minced
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons garlic powder
  • 1/2 teaspoon ground black pepper
  • 1/2 cup parsley chopped
  • 1 1/2 teaspoons salt
  • 1 tablespoon sesame oil
  • 2 eggs
