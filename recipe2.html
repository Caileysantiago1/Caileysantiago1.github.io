My Moms Lumpia Recipe

Lumpia are a type of spring roll from Filipino cuisine, consisting of a thin
wrapper filled with savory or sweet ingredients like ground pork and vegetables.
They can be served fresh or deep-fried until crispy and are often enjoyed as an
appetizer or snack at gatherings.

Lumpia has its origins in ancient China, introduced to the Philippines by Chinese
immigrants centuries ago. The name comes from the Hokkien words "lun" (moist)
and "pia" (pastry). Over time, it became a staple in Filipino cuisine.

  • Ingredients
  • 1 1/2 lbs ground pork
  • 2 onions, minced
  • 2 carrots, minced
  • 1 1/2 tsp garlic powder
  • 1/2 cup parsley, chopped
  • 1 1/2 tsp salt
  • 1 tbsp sesame oil
  • 2 eggs
  • Instructions
  • Combine all the filling ingredients in a bowl, then mix until well blended.
  • Wrap the mixture in egg roll wrappers or traditional lumpia wrappers.
  • Deep fry until brown and crispy.
